PROJECT ANN

Project Ideology:

“Ann” means “food” and our project aims to ameliorate the economic, social and safety conditions of roadside food stalls (popularly known as dhabas) across New Delhi. The target audience comprises of 3 communities namely the owners of the dhaba, the workers of the dhaba and the customers who eat there. 

Success Story:

Project Ann is a unique endeavour for improving one of the most fundamental businesses in India. This project incorporates the profit one-profit all approach to alleviate the problems faced by the target audience. The sustainability of the project is ensured by a chain reaction, initiated by healthy competition on the local level. Ask Mr. Balaji of Shri Balaji Dhaba in South-Delhi, and his experience is testament to the fact that basic hygiene measures, an entrepreneurial outlook and incentives for trained workers to retain them have transformed his venture from a deserted dhaba into a thriving restaurant. At the same time, Project Ann has given a place for customers to have hygienic food and provided financial stability to workers.

How we will make it Bigger:

From helping entrepreneurs to creating entrepreneurs. That is where Project Ann aims to go in the next one year. Tapping the potential of the large number of uneducated and unemployed youth, we aim to set up mobile vans which will sell clean and reasonably priced food for customers. This will ensure employment generation as well as hygienic food for the community.
