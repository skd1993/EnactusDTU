PROJECT UNMOOLAN

Project Ideology:

Unmoolan in Hindi means “eradication”. This project not only hopes to eradicate norms of social and economic inequality of women in Indian society, but also, health problems existing in rural communities across the city.  Project Unmoolan is basically an effort to improve the lifestyle of the people of slums and adjoining areas.

Success Story:

It’s very depressing to know that of all the rich G20 nations, India has been labelled the worst place to live for a woman. Therefore, we decided to create a project that will make women more self-sustainable and promote healthier living through our actions. We empowered the women of the slum by providing them with a regular source of income and training them in basic first aid. We have also improved the basic health and hygiene of the slums by selling the dwellers subsidised medicines and organising regular Health Camps. Through this project, we have generated an income of Rs. 1000-1800 per woman entrepreneur in the slums of Badli and others. Around 2035 people have been registered till now and about 24 entrepreneurs established.

How we will make it Bigger:

In the 21st century we all talk about being independent, and even though there has been a paradigm shift in the thought process of people, nothing concrete has been shaped for the upliftment of women. This project will specifically strive to remove the demeaning, degrading and negative connotations associated with women in our society. Furthermore, by educating women and helping them sell and earn for themselves will boost the morale of the other underprivileged people who have the potential to grow and work but due to societal and family constraints are bounded within the four walls of their homes.
